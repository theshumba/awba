Privacy notice

You gave us one thing. Here’s exactly what we do with it.

Joining the Awba waitlist means trusting us with an email address before we’ve shipped anything. We take that seriously — this page says plainly what we collect, why, and how to take it back.

Last updated 3 July 2026 · applies to the waitlist at awba.app

What we collect

  • Your email address — the one you type into the waitlist form.
  • A timestamp — when you signed up and when you confirmed.
  • A consent record — proof that you ticked the box and clicked the confirmation email, so we never have to guess whether you asked to hear from us.

That’s the whole list. No tracking pixels on this site, no analytics profile, no address books uploaded on your behalf.

Why we collect it

One purpose: to email you when Awba’s early access opens. We won’t use the list for anything else, and we won’t email you about other products or “updates you might like”.

Our lawful basis is your consent (UK GDPR, Article 6(1)(a)), and we follow the UK’s electronic-marketing rules (PECR). That’s why the consent box is never pre-ticked, and why we ask you to confirm by email before you’re on the list at all — a double opt-in.

Who holds it

The list lives with MailerLite (MailerLite Inc., with EU-hosted infrastructure), the service we use to store signups and send the launch email. MailerLite processes your address only on our instructions; their own privacy terms are at mailerlite.com/legal/privacy-policy.

Nobody else gets it. We don’t share the list with advertisers, partners, or anyone who’d call it an “audience”.

How long we keep it

Until the launch emails have gone out and the waitlist has done its job, after which the list is deleted. If you withdraw before then, we remove your address within 30 days. If you start a signup but never click the confirmation email, that unconfirmed record is deleted rather than kept around.

Your rights

  • Withdraw anytime — every email we send has an unsubscribe link, or just write to us. Withdrawing is instant and doesn’t need a reason.
  • Access — ask us what we hold about you (it will be the three items above).
  • Correct or erase — ask us to fix your address or delete it entirely.
  • Complain — if you think we’ve handled your data badly, you can complain to the UK regulator, the Information Commissioner’s Office. We’d rather you told us first, so we can put it right.

To use any of these, email salaam@awba.app.

And the line that matters most: we never sell your data. Not the list, not “insights” from it, not to anyone, not ever. An app built around trust doesn’t get to trade in it.
